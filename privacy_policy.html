Data Sovereignty & Storage Architecture:
Acro Scan operates on a strict "Local-First" architecture. Aelvra does not own, operate, or maintain external cloud servers for the storage of your documents. All data generated, scanned, or processed by the Application resides exclusively in two locations:
  1. Local Device Storage: The internal memory of your mobile device.
  2. Your Personal Google Drive™: Only if you explicitly authenticate and enable cloud synchronization features.

1. Information Collection and Device Permissions

To facilitate the core functionality of the Application, we require access to specific hardware and software features on your device. We do not collect this data for marketing, tracking, or advertising purposes.

  • Camera Access: The Application requires permission to access your device's camera sensor strictly for capturing images of documents. This data is processed locally in real-time and stored only on your device.
  • Storage Access (Read/Write): The Application requires access to your device's storage to:
    • Save scanned documents in multiple formats (images, PDFs, and proprietary .acro archive files)
    • Import existing images for editing and document creation
    • Create and manage a local database of your scans using SQLite
    • Store user preferences (such as theme settings and camera modes)
  • Device Orientation Access: The Application uses device orientation sensors to automatically detect and adjust document orientation during scanning for optimal capture and processing.
  • Internet Access: The Application requires internet permission exclusively for:
    • Google Drive API authentication and file synchronization
    • No other network activity, analytics, advertising, or data transmission occurs

2. Data Processing and Local Operations

All document processing occurs locally on your device. The Application performs the following operations entirely offline:

Local Processing Activities:
  • Image Processing: Cropping, rotation, filtering, and enhancement of scanned images
  • PDF Generation: Conversion of images to PDF format using the local PDF library
  • Archive Creation: Packaging of multiple images into proprietary .acro format (compressed archive with metadata)
  • Database Storage: Maintaining a local SQLite database of your scans, metadata, and organization
  • Preference Storage: Saving your app settings (theme, camera mode) using SharedPreferences
  • Image Sharing: Preparing documents for sharing with other apps on your device

Important: None of these operations transmit data outside your device unless you explicitly choose to sync with Google Drive or share files with other apps.

3. Use of Personal Data and Content

Your content is yours. Our use of data is strictly limited to providing the utility of the Application:

  • Document Content: We do not have access to, nor do we view, analyze, or process the contents of your scanned documents on our servers, as we do not maintain such servers.
  • Google Sign-In Data: If you choose to link your Google Drive, we use Google Sign-In solely to authenticate your identity. Authentication tokens are stored securely on your device using encrypted storage (FlutterSecureStorage/Android Keystore) and are used only to facilitate direct communication between your device and Google's servers.
  • Application Logs: The Application maintains local diagnostic logs for debugging and error tracking purposes. These logs may include:
    • Application lifecycle events (start, stop, errors)
    • Cloud sync operation status (success/failure, without document content)
    • Camera initialization events
    • File operation timestamps
    These logs are stored locally on your device and are never transmitted to external servers or third parties.

4. Cloud Synchronization and Google Drive™

The Application integrates with the Google Drive API to provide optional backup and synchronization services. By enabling this feature, you acknowledge and agree to the following:

  • Direct Transfer: Files are transferred directly from your device to your personal Google Drive via encrypted protocols (HTTPS/TLS). Aelvra does not intercept, store, or have access to these files.
  • Folder Structure: The Application creates a dedicated "Acro" folder in your Google Drive. Within this folder:
    • Each scan is stored as a separate subfolder
    • Images are uploaded with sequential naming (Acro_Scan_1, Acro_Scan_2, etc.)
    • Incremental sync uploads only new images to avoid duplication
  • Limited Use Disclosure: The Application's use and transfer to any other app of information received from Google APIs will adhere to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.
  • Google Privacy Policy: Data stored in Google Drive is subject to Google's Privacy Policy and Terms of Service. We encourage you to review them here: Google Privacy Policy.
  • Two-Way Sync: If you rename folders in the Application, these changes may be reflected in your Google Drive folder structure.

5. Third-Party Services and Libraries

The Application utilizes trusted open-source libraries and third-party frameworks to ensure stability, performance, and functionality. These components operate locally on your device and do not transmit your personal data or document content to external parties:

Third-Party Components:
  • Google Sign-In SDK: For secure OAuth authentication with Google Drive
  • Image Processing Libraries: For cropping, filtering, and enhancing scanned images (processed locally)
  • PDF Generation Library: For converting images to PDF format (processed locally)
  • Share Plus: For sharing documents with other apps on your device
  • SQLite Database: For local storage of scan metadata and organization
  • Archive Library: For creating compressed .acro archive files (processed locally)

Note: Image cropping functionality may use platform-specific native libraries. All processing remains on your device.

6. Data Security Measures

We implement robust security standards to protect your data locally:

  • Secure Credential Storage: Sensitive credentials (such as OAuth tokens for Google Drive) are stored using platform-native secure storage mechanisms:
    • Android: FlutterSecureStorage with Android Keystore
    • iOS: Keychain Services
  • Encryption in Transit: All network requests made by the Application to Google Drive are encrypted using SSL/TLS protocols (HTTPS).
  • Local Data Protection: Documents stored on your device are protected by your device's security features (device encryption, screen lock, etc.).
  • No Analytics or Tracking: The Application does not use any third-party analytics, crash reporting, or tracking services that would transmit your data externally.

While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your data, no method of transmission over the internet or method of electronic storage is 100% secure. You are responsible for maintaining the security of your device and Google account credentials.

7. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do not share, sell, rent, or trade your personal information or document content with third parties. The only data sharing that occurs is:

  • User-Initiated Sharing: When you explicitly use the "Share" feature to send documents to other apps on your device
  • Google Drive Sync: When you enable cloud synchronization and files are transferred to your personal Google Drive account
  • Legal Compliance: If required by law or to protect our rights, we may disclose information in response to lawful requests by public authorities

8. Data Retention and Deletion

You have complete control over your data:

  • Local Data: All scans and documents remain on your device until you explicitly delete them from the Application
  • Cloud Data: Files in Google Drive remain until you delete them from your Drive account
  • Account Disconnection: Disconnecting Google Drive removes authentication tokens from your device but does not delete files already stored in your Drive
  • App Uninstallation: Uninstalling the Application will remove all local data from your device. Cloud data in Google Drive will remain accessible through your Google account
